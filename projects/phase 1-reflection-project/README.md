# Phase 1 Reflection Project

The Phase 1 Reflection Project is the capstone assignment for Phase 1 — Foundations. It asks you to pull together everything from weeks 1–7 and show that you understand how PKI works as a connected system, not just as a list of topics.

## Before You Begin

Review all of your weekly reflections (`reflections/week-01.md` through `reflections/week-07.md`) before writing. Your submission should build on those entries — not repeat them or start from scratch.

## What You're Being Asked to Do

Work through five parts in the template:

1. **Your Phase 1 Journey** — how your understanding of PKI changed from Week 1 to now
2. **How the Pieces Connect** — walk through the full architecture: cryptographic foundations, certificates, trust, lifecycle, troubleshooting, and real-world deployment
3. **A Lab That Made It Real** — one lab from any week, with specific repo file path
4. **Explaining PKI to Someone Who Doesn't Know It** — explain what PKI actually is beyond "SSL certificates"
5. **Where You Go From Here** — reflect on the PKI Career Landscape from Week 7 and what you want to explore in Phase 2

You must also reference a **second lab** (different from Part 3) and include a **required visual** (see below).

## Submission Formats

### Choose one:
- Written reflection — 500–800 words
- Video explanation — 5–7 minutes (paste your link in the template)

## Required Visual

Your visual must show PKI as a connected system. Choose one:
- A TLS handshake with the full certificate chain labeled at each step
- A complete certificate lifecycle from key generation through revocation
- A PKI trust hierarchy with root CA, intermediate CA, and leaf certificate annotated

## Where to Submit

Commit the completed template to your GitHub portfolio repository:

```
reflections/phase1-reflection.md
reflections/visual/phase1-diagram.[png | pdf | jpg]
```
